Eye Contact

Principle: Short glances are less intense than staring. Example: smile briefly then glance away.

Conversation Openers

Principle: Use factual questions. Example: "What project are you working on today?"

Small Talk Exit

Principle: Use a short phrase and body language to end politely. Example: "Excuse me, I need a short break."

Active Listening

Principle: Reflect back key words. Example: "So the meeting is at 3?"

Workplace Overwhelm

Principle: Prioritize & break tasks into small steps. Example: "I'll do X first, then Y next."

Sensory Triggers

Principle: Control the environment when possible. Example: wear headphones or choose a seat away from noise.

Masking & Adaptation

Principle: Use minimal actions that feel safe. Example: small nods or neutral phrases.

Relationship Communication

Principle: Be direct about needs. Example: "I need quiet time after social events to recharge."
