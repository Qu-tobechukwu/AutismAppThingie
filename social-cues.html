Social Cues

This is the Social Cues Page. Select a situation type to see tips and examples.

Workplace

  • Small Talk: Keep it short; comment on the weather or shared task. Example: "Good morning! How was your weekend?"
  • Requests: Ask politely and clearly; avoid overexplaining. Example: "Could you clarify the deadline for this task?"
  • Feedback: Listen actively, thank the person, avoid defensive responses.

Daily Life

  • Greeting People: Smile lightly, say hello. Example: "Hi! How are you?"
  • Waiting in Line: Keep body relaxed, avoid fidgeting excessively.
  • Small Talk in Stores: Comment on product or environment briefly.

Events / Social Gatherings

  • Introductions: Offer name, handshake optional. Example: "Hi, I’m [Name]. Nice to meet you."
  • Joining Conversations: Listen first, then contribute small, relevant comment.
  • Exiting Conversations: Use polite exit phrases. Example: "Excuse me, I need to get some water."
