Eye Contact

Principle: Short glances are less intense than prolonged staring. Example: smile at a colleague and glance away when you feel comfortable.

Conversation Openers

Principle: Use factual questions. Example: "What project are you working on today?" - simple and safe.

Small Talk Exit

Principle: Use body language and short phrases to end politely. Example: "Excuse me, I need a quick break."

Active Listening

Principle: Reflect back a short phrase. Example: "So the meeting is at three?" ‚Äî this shows attention.

Workplace Overwhelm

Principle: Prioritize & break tasks into single steps. Example: "I'll do X first, then Y next." Ask for clarification on priorities.

Sensory Triggers

Principle: Control environment where possible. Example: wear headphones, choose seat away from noise sources.

Masking & Adaptation

Principle: Use minimal social behaviours that feel safe. Example: small nods or neutral phrases to blend without exhausting yourself.

Relationship Communication

Principle: Be direct about needs. Example: "I need some quiet time after social events to recharge."
